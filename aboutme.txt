My journey into design began at community college, where I discovered a passion for blending creativity with analytical thinking — taking every class available and earning my AA in Graphic Design. Rather than enter the workforce, I bet on myself: working seven days a week to afford California College of the Arts, I built a strong portfolio and earned a scholarship to transfer in as a sophomore.

Alongside school, I worked as an optician at a well‑known San Francisco practice, where I learned the art of customer service, trust, and communication. That experience shaped my understanding of empathy and how customer needs connect to business goals — lessons that became foundational to my UX career. I still maintain a relationship with the practice today.

At CCA, I was surrounded by inspiring teachers and classmates who pushed my thinking — exploring behavior, experience design, prototyping, and storytelling across digital and physical experiences. Two projects stood out: a CCA × Google design workshop and a founder class on building a startup through design thinking. I set a goal to land a UX internship as a junior, and through persistence, I landed one at Walmart.

After graduation — having fully funded my own education — I accepted a full‑time role at Walmart International. Over five years, I worked across the Canada, Mexico, and Chile markets on the eCommerce platform, contributing to the customer journey end‑to‑end. My work spanned UX design, research, video creation, and culture‑building as a social captain, with many ideas validated by the market.

Today, I’m excited for what’s next — ready to lead new design initiatives, bring thoughtful creativity to a team, and keep growing as a strong individual contributor. I’m driven by empathy, curiosity, and a desire to create meaningful experiences.
