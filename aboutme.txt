My journey into design began at my local community college, where I discovered a passion for blending creativity with analytical thinking. I immersed myself fully — taking every class available, earning multiple certificates, and completing my AA in Graphic Design. When it came time to choose between entering the workforce or continuing my education, I decided to bet on myself. Tuition at California College of the Arts was a stretch, but by working seven days a week and building a strong portfolio, I earned a scholarship and transferred in as a sophomore.

Alongside school, I worked as an optician at a well‑known practice in San Francisco. It was there that I learned the art of customer service and the importance of trust. I developed strong communication skills and a genuine love for serving my community. That experience shaped my understanding of empathy, interpersonal relationships, and how customer needs connect to business goals — lessons that later became foundational to my UX career. I still maintain an active relationship with the practice today.

At CCA, I was surrounded by inspiring teachers and classmates who pushed my thinking. I explored behavior, experience design, prototyping, storytelling, and emerging technologies — applying them to both digital and physical experiences. Two projects were especially memorable: a CCA × Google design workshop and a creative founder class where I learned how to build a startup through design thinking. I set a personal goal to earn a UX internship as a junior, and through persistence and growth, I landed an internship at Walmart.

After graduation, I achieved another milestone: I fully funded my education myself and accepted a full‑time role at Walmart International. Over the next five years, I worked across the Canada, Mexico, and Chilean markets on the eCommerce platform, contributing to the customer journey end‑to‑end. My work spanned UX design, research, video creation, and even culture‑building as a social captain. Many of my ideas came to life — and many were validated by the market.

Today, I’m excited for what comes next. I’m ready to lead new design initiatives, bring thoughtful creativity to future teams, and continue growing as a strong individual contributor. I’m driven by empathy, curiosity, and a desire to create meaningful experiences — and I’m eager to add value wherever I apply my skills.
